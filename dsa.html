Social Progress vs. Demographic Decline in Türkiye (2009-2024)

An analysis of how urbanization and education influence reproductive and marital trends.

Design Decisions & Personal Reflection

Encoding & Normalization Strategy

For this task, I decided to implement a Normalized Index Chart because comparing absolute population counts (millions) with literacy rates (percentages) in a single view is traditionally problematic. By setting 2009 as my baseline (Index 100), I successfully shifted the focus from "how many" to "how fast." In terms of Marks and Channels, I used lines to represent temporal change, where the spatial position on the Y-axis encodes the relative growth velocity. This choice maximizes expressiveness, allowing us to see the "Scissors Effect"—the dramatic divergence between social development and demographic cooling.

Gestalt Principles in Action

I didn't just want a pretty chart; I wanted it to be functional. I heavily relied on the Law of Proximity by using Direct Labeling. By placing labels at the very end of each line path, I eliminated the traditional legend. This design choice drastically reduces cognitive load, as the viewer's eye no longer needs to jump back and forth between a color box and the data. Furthermore, I utilized the Law of Similarity to categorize my data: I grouped city populations with dashed lines to provide context, while using thick solid lines for my primary research indicators to draw immediate attention to the core story.

Practical Usefulness & Insight

This visualization is particularly useful for sociologists and urban planners. It provides a "big picture" view of Türkiye’s changing social fabric. One can clearly observe that as Muğla experiences rapid urbanization and the national Literacy Rate climbs, the Birth and Marriage rates experience an almost symmetrical decline. This inverse correlation suggests that increased social awareness and urban living costs are primary drivers of demographic shifts. The use of High-Contrast Hue encoding ensures that each city is uniquely identifiable, preventing visual interference in crowded areas of the plot.

Strengths & Critical Limitations

The primary strength of this design is its ability to reveal hidden correlations between disparate variables. However, a notable weakness is the sacrifice of absolute precision. While the viewer can easily see that Muğla grew by ~34%, they cannot determine the exact population count without interacting with the tooltips. I chose to accept this trade-off because my research question focuses on trends and influence rather than exact census figures. This prioritization of Effectiveness over raw data density makes the visualization more accessible for storytelling.
